## Step 1 ŌĆö Find an Attackable Contract

On BattleChain, any contract in **attack mode** (state `3` ŌĆö `UNDER_ATTACK`) is a legitimate target under Safe Harbor.

**For this quickstart**, you'll attack a pre-built vulnerable vault from the starter repo. In the real world, you'd find targets through the explorer or on-chain queries.

<Tip>
**Finding real targets:** Browse all currently attackable agreements on the [BattleChain explorer](https://explorer.testnet.battlechain.com/agreements?page=1&state=UNDER_ATTACK). To check a specific contract programmatically, use `isAttackable(address)` from `battlechain-lib` ŌĆö it handles the full scope hierarchy including child contracts. See [How to Find Attackable Contracts](/battlechain/how-to/find-attackable-contracts) for details and usage examples.
</Tip>

If you just completed [Deploy and Battle-Test Your Contract](/battlechain/quickstart/deploy-your-contract), your vault is already set up ŌĆö skip to Step 2.

Otherwise, you need to deploy a vault first. The quickest way is to follow the [deploy quickstart](/battlechain/quickstart/deploy-your-contract) and come back here when your vault is in attack mode (state `3`).

## Step 2 ŌĆö Check the Terms

Before attacking any contract, read the agreement terms. For the starter vault the terms are straightforward ŌĆö but on real targets, this step is critical.

Key things to check:

- **Bounty percentage** ŌĆö how much you keep (starter vault: 10%)
- **Recovery address** ŌĆö where you send the remaining funds
- **Retainability** ŌĆö whether you keep the bounty from drained funds directly, or return everything and get paid separately
- **Identity requirements** ŌĆö whether the protocol requires you to identify yourself
- **Scope** ŌĆö which contracts are covered by the agreement

<Tip>
You can view all agreement details on the [BattleChain explorer](https://explorer.testnet.battlechain.com/agreements?page=1&state=UNDER_ATTACK) ŌĆö click any agreement to see its terms, covered contracts, and recovery address. The [explorer API](https://block-explorer-api.testnet.battlechain.com/docs) also exposes these details via `GET /battlechain/agreement/:agreementAddress`. For a full reference, see [Bounty Terms](/battlechain/reference/bounty-terms).
</Tip>

## Step 3 ŌĆö Understand the Exploit

Open `src/Attacker.sol` in the starter repo. The attack exploits two things working together: the CEI violation in `VulnerableVault` and the hook system in `MockToken`.

`just attack` deploys a thin `Exploit` wrapper (`src/Exploit.sol`) whose constructor deploys this `Attacker`, approves attack mode via the testnet moderator, and runs the exploit ŌĆö all in one transaction.

**MockToken's hook system:** Any address can register a hook contract via `setTransferHook(address hook)`. When tokens are transferred *to* that address, the token calls `hook.onTokenTransfer()` after the transfer completes.

**The vault's CEI violation:** `withdrawAll()` calls `TOKEN.transfer()` *before* zeroing `balances[msg.sender]`.

Put them together and the reentrancy chain looks like this:

```
attack()
ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ TOKEN.setTransferHook(address(this))  ŌåÉ register as our own hook
ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ vault.deposit(seedAmount)             ŌåÉ establish a balance
ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ vault.withdrawAll()
    ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ TOKEN.transfer(attacker, amount)
        ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ onTokenTransfer()             ŌåÉ hook fires, balance not yet zeroed
            ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ vault.withdrawAll()
                ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ TOKEN.transfer(attacker, amount)
                    ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ onTokenTransfer() ŌåÉ still not zeroed
                        ŌööŌöĆŌöĆ ...           ŌåÉ repeats until vault is empty
```

Once the vault is drained, the Safe Harbor settlement runs automatically:

```solidity
uint256 total = TOKEN.balanceOf(address(this));     // 1,100 = vault's 1,000 + your 100 seed
uint256 recovered = total - SEED_AMOUNT;            // 1,000 ŌĆö the protocol's funds only
uint256 bounty = (recovered * BOUNTY_BPS) / 10_000; // 10% of recovered = 100

TOKEN.transfer(RECOVERY_ADDRESS, recovered - bounty); // return 900 to the protocol
TOKEN.transfer(BENEFICIARY, bounty + SEED_AMOUNT);    // keep 100 bounty + your 100 seed
```

The bounty is taken on the protocol's recovered funds only ŌĆö you don't earn a bounty on the seed you deposited, and you get that seed back. You're not stealing. The protocol gets the majority of funds back minus the agreed bounty. Everyone knew the rules when the agreement was signed.

## Step 5 ŌĆö Verify and Settle

<Check>
Search your `VAULT_ADDRESS` on the [BattleChain explorer](https://explorer.testnet.battlechain.com/) ŌĆö you'll see the attack transaction, the vault balance drop to zero, and the bounty transfer to your wallet.
</Check>

### Verify the math

The agreement set a 10% bounty (`BOUNTY_BPS = 1_000`). To drive the reentrancy, the exploit first deposits 100 tokens of its own as seed, so 1,100 gets drained (the vault's 1,000 + your 100 seed). The bounty is 10% of the **recovered protocol funds** ŌĆö you don't earn a bounty on your own seed:

- Recovered (protocol funds): `1,100 ŌłÆ 100 seed = 1,000 tokens`
- Bounty (yours): `1,000 ├Ś 10% = 100 tokens`
- Returned to protocol: `1,000 ŌłÆ 100 = 900 tokens`
- Your 100-token seed is returned to you on top, so your wallet nets **+100**.

If the numbers don't look right, check that `RECOVERY_ADDRESS` in your `.env` matches the address in the agreement ŌĆö that's where the returned funds go.

## Troubleshooting

### Forge scripts failing

Add `--skip-simulation` to any failing `forge script` command. Forge's local gas estimation doesn't work reliably on BattleChain.

<Tab title="AI CLI (foundry)">

Tell your AI: *"All forge script commands need the --skip-simulation flag."*

</Tab>

<Tab title="Manual (foundry)">

Add `--skip-simulation` to every `forge script` call.

</Tab>

### Stuck pending transaction

<Tab title="AI CLI (foundry)">

Tell your AI: *"I have a stuck transaction at nonce [N]. Send a replacement with a higher gas price using cast send with --value 0 to clear it."*

</Tab>

<Tab title="Manual (foundry)">

Send a replacement transaction to clear the stuck nonce:

```bash
# Get current base fee
cast gas-price --rpc-url %%testnet.rpc%%

# Send a replacement with a higher max fee (e.g. 2x the current base fee)
cast send \
  --account battlechain \
  --rpc-url %%testnet.rpc%% \
  --nonce <stuck-nonce> \
  --max-fee-per-gas <2x-current-base-fee> \
  --value 0 \
  0x0000000000000000000000000000000000000000
```

Find your pending nonce: `cast nonce --rpc-url %%testnet.rpc%% <your-wallet-address>`

</Tab>

### Out-of-gas failures

If a `forge script` command fails with a vague error even with `--skip-simulation`, try adding `-g 300` to use 3x the estimated gas.

</Tabs>
